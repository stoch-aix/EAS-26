Density Transformation Formula

In system simulation, we often generate a simple Uniform random number $X \sim U(0,1)$ and transform it to follow a target distribution $Y = g(X)$.

The Transformation: $Y = X^2$

If $X$ is uniform on $[0,1]$, then $f_X(x) = 1$. Let $g(x) = x^2$. To find $f_Y(y)$:

  1. Inverse function: $x = g^{-1}(y) = \sqrt{y}$
  2. Derivative: $\frac{dx}{dy} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{y}}$
  3. Result: $$f_Y(y) = 1 \cdot \left| \frac{1}{2\sqrt{y}} \right| = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{y}}$$
