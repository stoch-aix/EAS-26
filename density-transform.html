Density Transformation: From Uniform to Somewhere...

Random number generators produce $U[0,1]$-distributed realizations of some random variable $X$. The density transformation formula allows to compute the density of some transformation $g(X)$.

1. Source Distribution: $X \sim U(0,1)$ $$f_X(x) = \begin{cases} 1 & x \in [0, 1] \\ 0 & x \notin [0, 1] \end{cases}$$ 2. Mapping: $Y = X^2$. For $x \in [0, 1]$, the target domain is $y \in [0, 1]$. 3. Resulting Density: For $y \in (0, 1]$: $$f_Y(y) = f_X(\sqrt{y}) \cdot \left| \frac{1}{2\sqrt{y}} \right| = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{y}}$$ For $y \le 0$ or $y > 1$, $f_Y(y) = 0$.

Input Density $f_X(x)$

Output Density $f_Y(y)$
